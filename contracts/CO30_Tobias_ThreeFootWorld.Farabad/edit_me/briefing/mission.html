At 06:18, an incident occurred at Port Farabad when a shipping container fell from a crane, toppling several adjacent units. Within minutes, dock workers in the immediate area reported severe respiratory distress, with several collapsing on site. First responders arrived at 06:33, with the gendarmerie establishing an initial cordon while ambulance personnel treated multiple victims exhibiting chemical‑burn–like injuries. At 07:24, the cordon was expanded and port operations ceased after agents from the Chemical Incident Response Authority (CIRA) identified damaged fuel tanks inside one of the toppled containers. These tanks were found to be leaking an unknown hazardous chemical and were rigged with improvised explosive charges. Operators from the Service for Special Intervention (SSI) were attached to the perimeter. Due to the presence of explosives, CIRA personnel were unable to complete casualty extraction or gather further on‑scene evidence. Initial field tests indicate the leaking substance contains a chlorine‑based component (Cl2). Given the nature of the incident and the discovery of improvised chemical dispersal devices, investigators believe additional shipping containers may contain makeshift chemical weapons intended for targets abroad. There is a credible risk that these containers may begin leaking due to improper storage, structural damage, or the corrosive nature of the chemicals involved. It is imperative that these explosives do not detonate. Detonation is expected to rapidly disperse the chemical agent over several hundred meters within seconds and may contaminate an even larger area depending on wind conditions. Synixe has been contracted to locate and disarm all explosives, retrieve remaining casualties, and lead the investigation into the origin and manufacture of these chemical weapons. Due to supply constraints, only four chemical detectors and six NBCD suits with APRs have been sourced for this operation.
