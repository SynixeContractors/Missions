At 06:18, a shipping container fell from a crane at Port Farabad, toppling several others. Within minutes, nearby dock workers reported severe respiratory distress, with several collapsing. First responders arrived at 06:33. The gendarmerie established a cordon while ambulance crews treated victims showing chemical‑burn–like injuries. At 07:24, the cordon was expanded and port operations halted after Chemical Incident Response Authority (CIRA) agents found damaged fuel tanks inside a toppled container. The tanks were leaking an unknown hazardous chemical and were rigged with improvised explosive charges. Service for Special Intervention (SSI) operators secured the perimeter. Due to the explosives, CIRA personnel could not complete casualty extraction or collect further evidence. Initial field tests indicate the leaking substance contains a chlorine‑based component. Given the improvised dispersal devices, investigators believe additional containers may hold makeshift chemical weapons intended for export. Poor storage, structural damage, or chemical corrosion may cause further leaks. Detonation of any device would rapidly disperse the agent over several hundred meters and potentially contaminate a wider area depending on wind. Synixe has been contracted to locate and disarm all explosives, recover remaining casualties, and investigate the origin of the chemical weapons. Due to supply constraints, only four chemical detectors and six NBCD suits with APRs are available.
