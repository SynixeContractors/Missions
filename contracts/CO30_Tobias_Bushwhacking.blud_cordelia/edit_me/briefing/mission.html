Years of internal conflict have crippled the region, and the remnants of war are scattered throughout Suonkomar Sahav. Although large scale hostilities have subsided, militia forces still maintain a presence in the north. Synixe has been contracted to deliver supplies and establish an aid station on the grounds of the destroyed church in Kampong Samraong. Unfortunately, the only viable route passes through contested territory near a known militia stronghold. The militia rely heavily on guerrilla tactics, emerging like ghosts from the rainforest, and are no strangers to the use of mines. Contractors are tasked with setting up two tents before aid workers arrive by helicopter: one serving as a makeshift infirmary, the other as a field distribution point for food and water. Three trucks have been loaded with tents, furniture, medical supplies, food, water, and fuel. A fourth truck carries a repair container stocked with tools and replacement parts. The rough terrain and the hot, humid climate make vehicle maintenance difficult. Breakdowns are common, and the harsh environment may cause vehicles to fail without warning. ! Warning ! Security forces will support the convoy with artillery, employing a series of creeping barrages to clear the route ahead. The convoy must hold at each designated phase line until the fire missions are complete.
