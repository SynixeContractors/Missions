The North African Army is facing significant disruptions in the region of Gunkizli. Two vital airfields have been rendered nearly inoperative due to the relentless attacks on their convoys by a militant group known as Jaysh al-Ustadha (Army of the Righteous). The militant group, led by the elusive and ruthless Ghassan al-Layl, also known as the "Night Lion", has effectively halted operations and supplies between the two airfields. Ghassan al-Layl, the leader of Jaysh al-Ustadha, is driven by a fierce desire to overthrow any foreign influence in the region and establish a self-sustained, militant-controlled state. His strategic ambushes and guerrilla warfare tactics have made him a formidable adversary. Intelligence reports suggest that his motives are deeply rooted in reclaiming what he believes to be the rightful autonomy of his people and eradicating any opposition to his vision. EMPLOYER Name: North African Military Location: Northeastern Airfield, Gunkizli The North African Army maintains security and stability in the region. ENEMY FORCES Name: Jaysh al-Ustadha (Army of the Righteous) Jaysh al-Ustadha is a militant organization with a stronghold in Gunkizli. They are well-equipped and employ guerrilla tactics to disrupt the North African Army's operations. Ghassan al-Layl (The Night Lion) is a relatively young and charismatic leader known for his thick beard and a distinctive golden rifle. His youth belies his tactical cunning and ferocity in combat and he's both feared and revered among his followers, making him a central figure in the militant operations. A picture of Ghassan al-Layl instructing Jaysh how to handle the various weapons available to them is shown in the mission briefing. CIVILIAN CONSIDERATIONS Ensure minimal collateral damage to civilians.
