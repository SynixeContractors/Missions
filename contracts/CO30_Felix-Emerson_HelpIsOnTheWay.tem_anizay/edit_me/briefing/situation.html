A regional crisis has been cooking up in the region of Anizay, and as a result, a civil war ensued. NATO had been assisting its allies and associates in evacuations in the weeks leading up to the major pullout, now all that is left is some remnants of a few UNA forces still stationed at FOB Nauzad and FOB Obeh. EMPLOYER Name: United States Department of State The United States Department of State has outsourced its security operations to Synixe Contractors due to a limitation in jurisdictions after the events leading up to the NATO pullout. ENEMY FORCES Name: Anizay Freedom Insurgency The Anizay Freedum Insurgency has been trying to gain leverage over both national and international sources for attempting to counter many new foreign relations and reforms in Anizay, as such, they have taken to violence as a means to promote their message and pursue a revolution. As such, they have started to go after some notorious VIPs in the region of Anizay who have been left behind during the NATO pullout, and subsequently, the start of UNA's recess in the region. They are often seen wearing robes and turbans, and seem to be equipped with surplus equipment consisting of small arms and some statics. FRIENDLY FORCES Name: United Assistance (UNA) Location(s): FOB Nauzad, FOB Obeh United Assistance has been in charge of peacekeeping operations in Anizay. As of recent events, their efforts have been diminished, and their evacuation operations have ceased, leading up to Synixe Contractors involvement. INDEPENDENT FORCES Name: Anizay Militia The Anizay Militia has been at war with the Anizay Freedom Insurgency ever since the discontent in the region reached its boiling point. They have access to armored vehicles and military equipment previously provided by other foreign nations in support of the ongoing course of Anizay. CIVILIAN CONSIDERATIONS Civilians are present in the area and may or may not be in support of either Synixe Contractors, UNA, Anizay Militia, or the Anizay Freedom Insurgency.
