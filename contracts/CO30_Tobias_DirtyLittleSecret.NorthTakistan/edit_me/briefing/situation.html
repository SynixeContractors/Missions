North Takistan remains deeply fractured following the fall of General Farouk Al-Mansur’s regime and the June 6 Revolution that sparked nationwide unrest. The nationalist Watani faction now controls much of the southern territory, enforcing strict rule through military discipline and aggressive patrols. In contrast, the revolutionary Six-Six brigade holds only a handful of urban centers in the northwest, relying on local support and foreign aid to maintain their foothold. The extremist Ikhwan as-Sayf operates in scattered cells across the region, launching attacks to destabilize all sides. The UN maintains a fragile peacekeeping presence, but its influence is limited. ENEMY FORCES Name: Ikhwan as-Sayf ("Brothers of the Sword") Location: Throughout the AO The "Brothers of the Sword," are a ruthless Islamist militant faction operating in fragmented cells across the region. Driven by a vision of establishing a khaliphate, they reject all as enemies of their cause. Their fighters are typically seen wearing black headgear and concealing their faces. Their equipment is largely scavenged from the remnants of fallen government armories, including AK-pattern rifles, RPGs, mortars, drones and heavy machine guns mounted on technicals. Despite their lack of formal supply chains, they are well-armed and tactically aggressive, often deploying VBIEDs and ambush tactics to destabilize both military and civilian targets. Their presence in the AO represents a volatile wildcard—unpredictable, uncompromising, and hostile to all. FRIENDLY FORCES Name: 6th June Martyr Brigade ("Six-Six") Location: Mantiq, Tarchi and Qorghan The 6th June Martyr Brigade, commonly referred to as Six-Six, is a revolutionary militia formed in the aftermath of the June 6 uprising against the former regime. Comprised of local fighters, defected soldiers, and political dissidents, the group champions democratic reform and civilian rule. Their fighters wear mismatched gear, often a mix of donated equipment and captured uniforms, but typically display green armbands. Weaponry includes small arms, drones and light vehicles, much of it acquired through battlefield salvage or foreign aid. Name: United Nations (UN) Location: Mantiq The United Nations Mission in Takistan (UNMITAK) operates as a neutral peacekeeping and humanitarian force in the region, tasked with monitoring ceasefires, protecting civilians, and facilitating aid delivery amid the ongoing conflict. INDEPENDENT FORCES Name: Al-Wataniyah Front ("the Watani") Location: East of Qorghan and south of the Sya Dara Iron Mine The Watani, are a nationalist paramilitary faction composed of former regime loyalists, ex-military officers, and hardline political operatives. Their primary goal is to restore centralized authority and suppress revolutionary movements, viewing both the Six-Six and foreign actors as threats to national sovereignty. Their equipment is largely scavenged from the remnants of fallen government armories, including AK-pattern rifles, RPGs, drones, and heavy machine guns mounted on technicals.They wear turqoise armbands with a golden lion on it. The Watani have entered uneasy ceasefires and backchannel deals when it suits their strategic interests. Their presence in the AO is marked by rigid checkpoints, aggressive patrols, and a zero-tolerance approach to dissent, making them a formidable force with unpredictable allegiances.
